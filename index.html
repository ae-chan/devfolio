Improving the KNN Algorithm

KNN is an algorithm used to impute values for unknown datapoints by averaging the values of their k nearest neighbours. Typically, the Euclidean distance formula is used to identify said neighbours, but this approach gives equal weighting to all factors. Instead, I constructed a new distance measure in which weights are assigned based on the variates' scaled regression Betas. This strategy was tested over 1,000 simulations on a known dataset, with randomly selected datapoints being imputed by the new algorithm each time. Findings indicated that the new algorithm improved relative bias, but came at the hefty cost of high run-time.

Instance-Based Learning, Regression, Classification, Simulations
Stock Price Forecasting

Prophet is Facebook's renowned time-series forecasting algorithm, which identifies unique trends segmented by the effects of seasonality, holiday and other regressors. Having realized that there were limited applications to the financial markets, I developed a tool which integrated Prophet with Yahoo Finance functionality. Requiring only two user-given parameters (a stock ticker symbol and forecast horizon), the tool generates forecasts and presents them on an interactive UI.

Prophet, Time-Series Forecasting, Yahoo Finance, API
COVID in China vs SARS Worldwide

A pilot project to experiment with R's most commercialized data visualization platform, ggplot. I compiled the WHO's reports on COVID-19 in China as well as those of SARS back in '03. After cleaning and aligning the two datasets on comparable timescales, I used ggplot to convey exactly how serious the situation was. Even in February 2020, the visualization had already portrayed the grim reality that death rates from COVID in China had far surpassed those of SARS worldwide.
